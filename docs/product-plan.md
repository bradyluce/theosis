# Product Plan

## Core product statement

Theosis is a mobile-first Orthodox reading and study app built around one central promise: search any verse and immediately reach patristic commentary.

## Non-negotiables

- verse-first commentary access
- Orthodox daily liturgical structure
- beautiful mobile UX
- no AI chatbot or chat-wrapper behavior
- scalable library and ingestion architecture

## Product pillars

### 1. Bible first

The Bible reader is the center of the product. Scripture should feel fast, readable, and touch-friendly. Verse interactions unlock commentary, related writings, cross references, notes, highlights, and sharing.

### 2. Orthodox daily rhythm

Daily should feel devotional rather than dashboard-like. It surfaces the saint of the day, feast and fast status, readings, and short liturgical context in a modular scrolling layout.

### 3. Library and research

Library unifies Fathers, saints, theologians, and works into a filterable, readable corpus. Direct commentary must remain visibly distinct from related theological material.

### 4. Search as product infrastructure

Search is not a thin filter UI. It needs explicit routing for verse references, intelligent ranking, alternate spellings, highlighted snippets, and blended results that can scale beyond mock content.

## Early-phase success criteria

- the app feels intentional on first load, especially on mobile
- the Bible reader is the strongest flow in the product
- a verse tap reveals relevant, well-organized supporting material
- search returns direct commentary ahead of merely related texts
- the information architecture already matches the long-term product

## Out of scope for phase 1

- live auth
- server-side persistence
- full corpus ingestion
- production search infrastructure
- jurisdiction-specific branding or language

## Visual direction

- quiet editorial surfaces
- strong typography with generous reading rhythm
- restrained gold accents used sparingly
- soft stone backgrounds and deep navy text
- light surfaces with subtle borders instead of heavy shadows
- no ornamental pseudo-Byzantine styling

## Future expansion targets

- multiple Bible translations and original-language comparison
- broader corpus coverage across Fathers and later Orthodox writers
- calendar preference selection
- saved notes, highlights, favorites, and patron-saint personalization
- account sync and native-mobile reuse of shared domain logic
