# Tier 3 — Deferred Acquisitions

**Status:** Consolidated planning document — items investigated for Phase 2 Tier 3 supplementary acquisition but **NOT acquired due to PD-English-source unavailability**.

**Date:** 2026-05-20
**Purpose:** Track items deferred to future phases when PD English translations become available (typically as US copyright expires under the 95-year rule, or via new PD-released translations).

---

## Summary

Of 8 candidate Tier-3 items investigated, **2 were acquired** (Wallis Budge Paradise + Evagrius Praktikos) and **6 were deferred**. This document records the deferred items, the reason for deferral, and the date at which PD status will likely become available.

---

## Deferred items

### 1. Athanasius — Letters to Serapion on the Holy Spirit

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Why deferred** | No pre-1930 complete English translation identified |
| **Standard English translation** | C. R. B. Shapland, *The Letters of Saint Athanasius concerning the Holy Spirit* (Philosophical Library, New York, 1951) — copyrighted |
| **Recent translation** | Mark DelCogliano, Andrew Radde-Gallwitz, Lewis Ayres, *Works on the Spirit: Athanasius's Letters to Serapion and Didymus's On the Holy Spirit* (Popular Patristics, SVS Press, 2011) — copyrighted |
| **PD availability date** | Shapland 1951 enters US public domain in **2047** (95-year rule) |
| **Workaround** | None — these letters are not in NPNF II/4 Athanasius (Schaff omitted them) |
| **Why it matters** | Athanasius's Letters to Serapion are the **first systematic Greek pneumatology** — they establish the Holy Spirit's full divinity in response to the Pneumatomachi heresy. Pairs theologically with Basil's *De Spiritu Sancto* (which we have). Critical for Trinitarian theology completeness. |

### 2. Gregory Nazianzen — De Vita Sua and Other Poems

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Why deferred** | No pre-1930 English translation of the autobiographical poems identified; most of Gregory's ~17,000 lines of poetry have **never been translated to English** |
| **Standard English translation** | Carolinne White, *Gregory of Nazianzus: Autobiographical Poems* (Cambridge Medieval Classics, 1996) — copyrighted |
| **Other modern** | Peter Gilbert, Jacob H. Friesenhahn (selected poems) — copyrighted |
| **PD availability date** | White 1996 enters US public domain in **2092** |
| **Workaround** | Possible: search for partial early-1900s English in obscure scholarly journals; lower-quality fragments may exist |
| **Why it matters** | Gregory's autobiographical De Vita Sua (1,949 lines) is his most personal text. His Carmina Moralia/Theologica/Historica are the **single largest body of poetry from any Father**. The absence is a significant gap for understanding Gregory the man. |

### 3. Chrysostom — Paschal Catechetical Homily

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Why deferred** | No clearly-PD English translation identified; all online versions (OCA, GOARCH, OrthodoxWiki, tertullian.org references) are modern adaptations of uncertain licensing |
| **Possibly PD source** | The text has been translated into English many times since 1850; a comprehensive Reformation-era translation may exist but is not yet identified |
| **PD availability date** | Various modern adaptations would enter PD on different dates; some 19th-century translations are PD but provenance is murky |
| **Workaround** | This is a **short text** (~500-700 words) — manual transcription from any PD source would be trivial; or commission a fresh translation from PG 59:721-724 |
| **Why it matters** | Read at **every Orthodox Pascha midnight service** — one of the most famous Chrysostom texts in liturgical use. The library cannot serve Pascha liturgically without this. |
| **Recommendation** | High-priority deferral. Investigate whether a Reformation-era English version exists in PD; failing that, consider commissioning a fresh translation. |

### 4. Antony the Great — Seven Letters

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Why deferred** | Only complete modern translation is Samuel Rubenson, *The Letters of St. Antony: Origenist Theology, Monastic Tradition and the Making of a Saint* (1995) — copyrighted |
| **PD availability date** | Rubenson 1995 enters US public domain in **2091** |
| **Manuscript transmission** | The original Coptic survives only in fragments; complete versions exist in Latin, Georgian, and Arabic. Each language has its own translation tradition. |
| **Possible PD workaround** | The 1644 Latin translation by Abraham Ecchellensis (from Arabic) is PD but Latin only. Translating Latin → English would be a separate scholarly project. |
| **Why it matters** | Antony's Letters are the **only direct primary-source writings of the founder of Christian monasticism** (everything else is Athanasius's biography). Major theological depth (Origenist-influenced cosmology, anthropology). |

### 5. Symeon the New Theologian — Complete works

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Why deferred** | All English translations are modern (1980s-1990s); copyrighted |
| **Standard English translations** | C. J. de Catanzaro, *Symeon the New Theologian: The Discourses* (Paulist Press, 1980); Alexander Golitzin, *St. Symeon the New Theologian: On the Mystical Life: The Ethical Discourses* (3 vols, SVS Press, 1995-1997); Daniel Griggs, *Divine Eros: Hymns of St. Symeon the New Theologian* (SVS Press, 2010) |
| **PD availability date** | de Catanzaro 1980 → 2076; Golitzin 1995 → 2091 |
| **Why it matters** | Symeon the New Theologian (949-1022) is one of the **three "Theologians"** in the Orthodox tradition (with John the Evangelist and Gregory Nazianzen). His mystical Discourses + Hymns are foundational for Orthodox spiritual experience. Direct ancestor of the hesychast tradition. |

### 6. Theodore the Studite — Catecheses + Letters

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Why deferred** | All English translations are modern (1981+); copyrighted |
| **Standard English translation** | Catharine Roth, *St. Theodore the Studite: On the Holy Icons* (SVS Press, 1981); Patricia A. McGinley (translator), various partial Catechesis translations; St. Anthony's Greek Orthodox Monastery has translated selected Catecheses |
| **PD availability date** | Roth 1981 → 2077 |
| **Why it matters** | Theodore the Studite (759-826) is the **theological architect of Orthodox monasticism** and the principal iconodule theologian of the Second Iconoclast Controversy (815-843). His Small Catechesis (small daily exhortations) and Large Catechesis are foundational Orthodox monastic literature, comparable to Cassian's Conferences. |

### 7. Helen Waddell — The Desert Fathers (1936)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Why deferred** | 1936 publication; US copyright extends through 2031 (95-year rule); not yet PD |
| **Status** | Published 1936 by Henry Holt (US) and Constable (UK); Waddell d. 1965 |
| **PD availability date** | **2031** (US 95-year rule) — only ~5 years away |
| **Why it matters** | Waddell's *Desert Fathers* is a beautifully-translated selection from the Latin *Vitae Patrum* (Rosweyde 1628). Complementary to Wallis Budge's Syriac-tradition Paradise. Includes Sayings, hermit biographies, and the famous "Old Man Antony" stories in elegant English. **Mark for Phase-4 acquisition in 2031.** |

## Available now via different sources

Two items in this list have **partial PD-acquirable alternatives** that are worth chasing if needed:

### A. Antony the Great — Life by Athanasius
We already have **Athanasius's Life of Antony** (2811 in our New Advent corpus). While this is hagiography rather than Antony's own writing, it's the canonical primary source for Antony's life and teaching.

### B. Symeon the New Theologian — Hymns of Divine Love (selections)
George Maloney (?) and other older Catholic-press editions exist; need verification of PD status. **Investigate Phase 4.**

## Acquisitions strategy

**Now (Phase 2 done):** 2 items acquired (Wallis Budge Paradise + Evagrius Praktikos)

**Phase 3 (next):** Stop chasing Tier 3 PD gaps and pivot to **parser pipeline development** OR to expanding into modern non-NPNF/ANF/PD sources that we may have other licensing arrangements for.

**Phase 4 (~2031 onward):** Re-evaluate as PD status expires:
- 2031: Helen Waddell's *The Desert Fathers* (1936) becomes PD
- 2047: Athanasius Letters to Serapion (Shapland 1951)
- 2076-2092: Symeon, Theodore Studite, Gregory Nazianzen poems, Antony Letters
- 2031-2092: Most of these become PD at predictable intervals

**Always available:** Commission fresh PD translations from PG/PL critical editions for specific high-value texts (e.g., Chrysostom Paschal Homily — a 500-word text could be translated and PD-released).
